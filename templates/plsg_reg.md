This is how I see the Python Learning Support Group operating, at least at present:

1. We will meet online, once a week, for 1 to 2 hours per session
2. At the end of each session, I will assign a few tasks
3. In the first session, I intend to show live demonstrations which the participants can follow on their machines
4. In subsequent sessions, we will focus more on resolving the problems being faced by the members
5. I will support the group through documents available on the Internet for reference at any time and a WhatsApp group
6. If there are problems facing a significant number of members, we can have structured hands-on sessions to address such problems

The framework of the <strong>PLSG</strong> intends to handhold beginners and set tasks and learning goals - but
	the learning is expected to be self guided. That is the reason I am calling it a <strong>Support Group</strong> and
	not a <strong>Workshop</strong>. It will run for as long as it remains effective and useful to its participants.
